The Table Project

Everyone deserves a seat at the table

At The Table Project, we believe small acts of care can make a big difference. From seasonal comfort bags to handwritten notes, we’re building quiet moments of kindness for those who need it most — one neighbor at a time.

What We Do

The Table Project creates simple, meaningful outreach for overlooked members of our communities. We put together care packages, write letters, and organize small efforts that remind people they’re not alone. Our work is quiet by design — thoughtful, consistent, and rooted in real human connection.

Why We Do It

We believe that kindness doesn’t need to be loud to matter. There are too many people who go unseen — kids, seniors, families in transition. We exist to remind them someone’s thinking of them. That’s it. No big statements, just simple acts of care that help people feel human again.



How You Can Help

At The Table Project, every bit of support counts. Whether you’re donating your time to pack care packages, writing notes of encouragement, or simply spreading the word, you’re making a difference. We’re always looking for volunteers to join our efforts, and every small action helps create a ripple of kindness in the community. Join us in showing others they matter — in ways both big and small.

Where We Are

Based in Ellijay, GA, The Table Project serves local communities through a network of volunteers committed to bringing small moments of care to those in need. Carolyn Mae Whitmore, our founder, works closely with local schools, shelters, and churches to identify where help is most needed. While our efforts are based here, the message of kindness and community stretches beyond borders.
